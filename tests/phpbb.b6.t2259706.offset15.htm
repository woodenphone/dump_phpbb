Brf wrote:Then why do all of the comparisons say that only Sprint has unlimited? All of the others with "unlimited" plans throttle you after a certain amount per month.


Not sure why the above statement was made as Tmobile isn't throttling on its $80 unlimited plan. In fact the execs have been boasting of people using 70GB a month on the plan.

On the lower plans they do. For example for $50 you get 1GB of unthrottled then they throttle, $60 gets you 3GB unthrottled then they throttle, etc. However the $80 is uncapped and unthrottled (the only plan on Tmobile that isn't throttled right now).

If you add multiple lines it gets cheaper per line. Also Tmobile (on any plan) never has bandwidth overages as they just throttle you down. Now don't get me wrong I am not here to convince anyone to switch if they are happy with their current carrier and plan.

I am just passing along information. I personally left AT&T for Tmobile's $30/month which gives me 5GB of unthrottled LTE speed then it downgrades when I go over.

All these prices are for US based persons. Plus Tmobile has some interesting freebies if you travel outside the US in that you get unlimited texts and data in over 100 countries free of charge. Also they don't include streaming music in your data limits. You can stream all the music you want on selected services (like Pandora) without burning through your data. Of course on the $80 plan it is all unlimited anyway.

However I will state it again if you like your carrier and plan that is good. I am just passing out information for what it is worth.
