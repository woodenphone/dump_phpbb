 Homebrew chain lube

After reading the recent chain lube tests and finding the Friction Facts formula on their site, curiosity got the best of me. I ordered some powdered MoS2 and PTFE and set about making a home brew. I'm not willing to deal with the hassles of waxing, so I decided to use synthetic motor oil as a base instead. The FF formula uses a pound of wax, so I calculated the volume of that and used an equal volume of 1:1 oil and OMS blend. I don't know if that's even a reasonable idea, but I needed a starting point.

The proportion of solids to liquids seemed rather high when I mixed it and the resulting lube is dark grey due to the MoS2, but it's very light-bodied and penetrates immediately upon application. Once it's wiped off, there is almost no indication of the dark lube visible on the chain.

WARNING: PLACEBO EFFECT ALERT

That said, it did feel very slick when spinning the drivetrain in the workstand. On the road, it does feel considerably smoother than Pro Link, especially under high loads when climbing. Although it doesn't damp noise the way heavy/waxy lubes do, the drivetrain is noticeably quieter and smoother when cross-chaining. After the initial 30 mile test ride, a light oil film had seeped out, but a quick wipe with a rag got rid of it.

I'll put some more miles on it this weekend and try it on Linda's bike, too. I'm cautiously optimistic that I'm going to like this stuff.

For those of you who have played with your own homebrew lubes, I have a couple of questions:

What proportion of oil to thinner did you like best? My 1:1 mixture seems like it may be more oil than is optimum.

Is there a quicker-drying thinner than OMS that's readily available and perhaps less noxious and toxic? I'm considering using denatured alcohol next time, but I haven't tested mixing it with oil yet.

I use about 4:1, but adjusted seasonally and for various off-road conditions. I mix by eye, so not very accurate. If it seems heavy enough to creep out of the chain after one or two rides, I add a bit of extra solvent to my bottle.

As for the solvent, I am using what I believe is hexane mixed with PTFE particulate.
It's what comes out of a large (10oz by weight) aerosol can of Dry spray lube (as in completely dry) used for sliding windows, doors and such. It is sold under both the Liquid Wrench and WD40 brands, and at least one other brand, sells for about $7 and is quite fast-drying.

I first spray about a fourth of the contents of the can into a large squeeze bottle and throw in a couple of small hex nuts to provide agitation for the solid PTFE.
Next, I "greenhouse" the squeeze bottle under a large clear plastic pitcher, and leave it on my front deck where it heats up in the sun and outgasses any remaining propellant. This is necessary to prevent my final solvent/oil mix from being expelled forcefully from my smaller squeeze bottle that I use for mixing in the oil and for dispensing the lube. It's like getting rid of the unwanted "carbonation", leaving the fast-drying hexane solvent behind. No significant volume/mass of the solvent is lost to the degassing process.
Lastly, I shake up the solvent/ptfe and add it to my smaller TriFlo squeeze bottle, along with a 25% fraction of oil and a couple of hex nuts in this smaller bottle.

So I end up with a blend that leaves a minimum of oil inside of the chain, and which supplement's the oil's lubricity with particulate PTFE, still at a pretty low cost.

What I like best about my lube (and the Tri-flo dispenser I put it in) is that I lube the chain in just ten seconds and don't always need to do anything else (such as wiping the chain) if I am in a hurry. My friends see this (and my clean chain) and have almost all tried to beg a bottle off of me. The bottle's dispensing tube length and flow rate from the cap's restricting orifice get's fine-tuned for a practical rate of flow that does the job neatly in one pass, perhaps two or three turns of the crank and yeah, about ten seconds.

I call my lube "lite".

Hexane was the preferred solvent in White Lightning and other popular dry lubes, so is relatively safe within the normal suggested usage.
Thanks for the warning about static, I never gave thought to that!

Hexane at higher exposure levels is known to cause peripheral neuropathy, so not for prolonged skin contact and certainly not for indoor use. It's fast-drying behavior allows one to bring their bike inside after a relatively short outdoor drying, perhaps fifteen minutes on a warm day, which is not so true of mineral spirits.

Most solvents like hexane do not produce such heavy vapors as gasoline, so don't have the particular problems associated with that, where heavy vapors might creep along the floor great distances while still at flammable levels of dilution.
And hexane by itself is also nowhere near the flash point of butane and other honey-oil-lab liqui-gasses, not that I would ever suggest using those except maybe as a direct aerosol dry application (or for making honey oil;-)).
At least we're not lubing our chains with nitroglycerine, or with radioactive isotopes.

 Lube

    Hexanes a BIG NO. Too flammable, expensive, and has other negative effects
    Ratio of "oil" to solvent is 2 to 4:1. Lower ratio, more "oil". More oil yields better wet weather performance, but more dirt pick-up. Higher solvent content, less dirt, but shorter duration, especially wet.
    OMS is best all-purpose solvent for "Oil-based Forum Lube". Dries relatively quick and not too expensive or nasty
    OMS can dissolve low-molecular weight, low melting waxes. Melt wax and add to OMS. Only add 1-2 wt% wax
    Denatured Alcohol may not solvate oil. Denatured Alcohol is a slightly polar solvent and oil is non-polar. My guess is it will look like oil in water. Solubility rules "Like dissolves Like".


The Mobil Oil/OMS forum lube is pretty decent for most riding conditions and is clean, easy to apply, and inexpensive. You can add some 80-90 wt gear oil, or low-melting microcrsytalline wax (1-2 wt% per oil content) to improve wet weather performance.
